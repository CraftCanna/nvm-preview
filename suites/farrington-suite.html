Farrington Suite

Historic Elegance, Modern Accessibility

Main Floor ADA-Accessible Suite

Victorian Luxury for Everyone

Named in honor of the Farrington family who were early residents of this grand Victorian, the Farrington Suite occupies a privileged position on the mansion's main floor. This thoughtfully designed suite proves that historic charm and modern accessibility can coexist beautifully.

The suite's most striking feature is its original ornamental plaster ceiling—intricate Victorian craftsmanship that has been preserved for over 130 years. Below, an emerald green decorative hearth adds a jewel-toned focal point that perfectly complements the room's period character.

As our only main-level accommodation, the Farrington Suite offers barrier-free access throughout. The ADA-compliant bathroom features a spacious walk-in shower with grab bars and a built-in bench, all enhanced by luxurious heated floors. Whether you require accessible accommodations or simply prefer to avoid stairs, the Farrington Suite delivers the full New Victorian Mansion experience without compromise.

Thoughtful Design

Accessibility Without Compromise

We believe everyone deserves to experience the magic of staying in a historic Victorian mansion. The Farrington Suite was carefully designed to provide full accessibility while preserving the elegant character that makes our B&B special.

Accessibility Features:

  • Main floor location—no stairs required
  • Wide doorways throughout the suite
  • Roll-in shower with grab bars and built-in bench
  • ADA-height toilet with grab bars
  • Accessible sink and vanity area
  • Ample maneuvering space
  • Easy access to common areas and dining room

Please contact us in advance if you have specific accessibility needs—we're happy to discuss how we can best accommodate you.

The Farrington Experience

Living History

The Farrington Suite offers a unique opportunity to immerse yourself in Victorian history. Gaze up at the ornamental plaster ceiling—the same patterns that delighted the mansion's original residents in 1894—and imagine the stories these walls could tell.

The emerald green decorative hearth serves as a stunning focal point, its rich color a nod to the Victorian love of bold, jewel-toned interiors. While decorative rather than functional, it anchors the room with period-appropriate elegance.

Your main floor location means easy access to all the mansion's public spaces. Step directly from your suite to the elegant dining room for breakfast, relax on the wrap-around porch overlooking Boyd Park, or stroll through the courtyard garden—all without navigating a single step.
